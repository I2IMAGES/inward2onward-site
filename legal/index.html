1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device (computer, tablet, phone) when you visit a website. They help websites remember your preferences, understand how you interact with a site, and sometimes deliver relevant communications. Some cookies are placed by us; others are placed by third-party services we use to operate the site.

2. What Cookies We Use

We use the following categories of cookies on inward2onward.com:

Category Provider Purpose Duration
Strictly Necessary Inward2Onward Required for the site to function — session management, security, and form integrity. Session
Analytics Google Analytics Tracks pseudonymized usage data — pages viewed, session duration, traffic sources — to help us improve the site. No personally identifiable information is associated. Up to 2 years
Functional / CRM Keap / Thryv Supports form submissions and contact tracking for client relationship management purposes. Up to 1 year
Payment Security Stripe Used during the checkout process to detect fraud and verify browser authenticity. Not used for advertising. Session / Short-term
What we do not use: We do not use advertising cookies, retargeting pixels, social media trackers, or cross-site behavioral tracking cookies of any kind.

3. Your Cookie Choices

You have control. Here are your options:

  • Browser Settings: Most browsers allow you to refuse or delete cookies through their settings menus. Note that disabling strictly necessary cookies may impair site functionality.
  • Google Analytics Opt-Out: Install the Google Analytics Opt-Out Browser Add-on at tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout.
  • Cookie Banner: When you first visit our site, you will be presented with a cookie consent notice. You may accept or decline non-essential cookies at that time. You may withdraw consent at any time by clearing cookies in your browser and revisiting the site.

California residents may also submit opt-out requests per the CCPA by contacting privacy@inward2onward.com.

4. Do Not Track

Some browsers transmit "Do Not Track" (DNT) signals. Because there is no universally accepted standard for how websites should respond to DNT signals, our site does not currently alter its data collection practices in response to DNT signals. We will revisit this position as industry standards evolve.
